III. Rationale and Tips

  • Weekend Focus: This schedule allows you to concentrate on each NPTEL course on separate weekend days, which can improve focus and retention.
  • Weekday Review: During the week, the schedule shifts to reviewing the NPTEL material covered on the weekend. This spaced repetition is crucial for long-term learning.
  • Morning vs. Evening: You've indicated a preference for doing the bulk of the NPTEL lectures in the morning and evening, which this schedule accommodates.
  • Flexibility: If you find you're consistently more productive with a slightly different arrangement, feel free to adjust the times, but keep the core principles of dedicated time for each course and regular review.
  • MST Exam Priority: Remember that your MST exams take priority. If you need more time for them, reduce the NPTEL review time during the week.
  • Catch Up: If you fall behind on the weekend, use some of your "Free" time during the week to catch up, but don't let it become a habit.
  • Active Learning: Don't just passively watch the lectures. Take detailed notes, try to solve the examples yourself, and think about how the concepts relate to real-world problems.
  • Consistency is Key: The most important thing is to stick to the schedule as consistently as possible. Even if you can't follow it perfectly every day, try to get back on track as soon as you can.
